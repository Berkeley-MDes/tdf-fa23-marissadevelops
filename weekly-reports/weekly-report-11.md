# Weekly Report 10: 11/3 - 11/9

## Project 3 Reflection

Throughout most of project 3, I was trying to manage potential burnout, so I definitely didn't get to put as much time and iteration into my chatbot as I would've liked. I was still very happy with the output though based on what I fed it, and I actually use my chatbot every couple days to help me with random academic tasks. For example, I asked it to write my MDSA Academic Officer biography for the application (although I ended up declining the opportunity anyways). I also used it to help draft an introduction of my research background for an outreach email to an education journal I want to publish for! Of course, I replace/reword around 50% of the content, but it helps me identify key points that I'd like to expand on. 

I really thought I was doing something when I edited my Project 3 video, but every time we get to presentation day in class, I never feel like I encapsulated my experience well enough. I think I capture the *narrative* of my projects, but I always get peer feedback asking for more technical details. Considering I upload it to my personal YouTube channel, I constantly consider my audience, which is often people who are not in academia or tech. My tone often leads more towards my philosophy around learning within the project instead of explicit details for how I built it.
